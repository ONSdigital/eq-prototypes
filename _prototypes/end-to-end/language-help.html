What is the census?

The census is a survey of all the people and households in England and Wales that looks at who we are and how we live. The census happens every 10 years and the next one takes place in 2021.

Your answers to the census questions will help organisations plan and fund public services in your area, including language services. For example, the NHS may need to provide translation and interpretation services.

The Office for National Statistics (ONS) plans and runs the census in England and Wales. The ONS is the UK’s largest independent producer of official statistics. We only use your census information for statistical purposes and none of your personal details are published.

Who has to complete the census

The census asks questions about you and your household and it’s important that everyone in England and Wales takes part.

!
You must complete the census by law

It is an offence to supply false information or to not complete the census, and you could be fined up to £1,000. Some questions are clearly labelled as voluntary. It is not an offence if you do not answer these.

Your personal information is protected by law and will be kept confidential

Staying in the UK for less than three months

You are not responsible for filling in the census if you are staying in the UK for less than three months. The owner of your accommodation will need to fill in the census about the household and the people staying there. They will need to answer one or two questions about you, for example, where you usually live.

When to complete your census

All households should complete the census on Sunday 21 March 2021 or as soon as possible after.

How to complete your census

You can choose to either:

  • fill in your census online
  • fill in your census on paper
